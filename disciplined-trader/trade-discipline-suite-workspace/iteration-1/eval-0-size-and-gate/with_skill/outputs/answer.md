# Trade Plan — Gate 3 (Plan & Size)

Here is your trade, with all parameters fixed *before* you touch the order ticket. The point of this is to move the decision out of the live, emotional moment — the plan decides, not the screen.

## Your four parameters

1. **Entry trigger** — $4.20. You said the stock is "running," so be precise about what your trigger actually is: a break *and hold* of $4.20 on volume, not a wick through it. If it can't hold, you don't have your entry.
2. **Invalidation / stop** — $3.95 (prior support that held). This is a valid stop: it's a *prior structural level*, not the breakout line, so the thesis is genuinely wrong if price loses it. Good — this is exactly where a stop belongs.
3. **Target** — $4.95 (where you scale out).
4. **Size** — computed below, not chosen by feel.

## Verdict: **GO** (plan math passes)

R:R is **3.00 : 1**, comfortably above the 2.0 minimum, and your size is non-zero. The plan clears Gate 3.

**Size discipline:** buy **320 shares**, no more. At $0.25 of risk per share, that caps your loss at $80 (1% of the account) if $3.95 breaks. Do not round up to "a nice 500 shares" — that quietly turns a 1% risk into 1.6% and breaks the math.

## Caveats before you click

- **GO here means the plan math is sound — not that you should trade.** You still must clear the other gates: Is the setup itself one of your A-setups (Gate 1/2)? Is your head right and are you within your daily/weekly loss limits (Gate 4 / state-check)? And the numbers above are **gross** — commissions, slippage on a small-cap (spreads can be wide and fills poor on something "running"), and taxes all eat into that 3R (Gate 5).
- **A running small-cap is exactly where slippage bites.** If you can't get filled near $4.20, the trade changes — re-run it, don't chase. Chasing a higher entry shrinks your reward and your R:R.
- If $4.20 won't hold, there is no trade. Wait for the trigger; don't anticipate it.
