# Should you take the GENI trade? Let's walk through it.

First, the honest framing: I'm not going to tell you "yes, buy it." This is an educational walk-through of your own setup against the numbers and the risks. The decision and the consequences are yours. With a low-float, news-driven small cap, the single most important thing is that the trade survives *your own rules*, not whether the story sounds good.

The good news up front: your risk parameters are actually sound. The danger isn't the math — it's the parts of the setup you haven't pinned down yet.

## The numbers (these are solid)

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Entry | $5.50 |
| Stop | $5.20 |
| Target | $6.40 |
| Risk / share | $0.30 |
| Reward / share | $0.90 |
| **Reward:Risk** | **3.0 : 1** |
| Breakeven win rate at 3R | **25%** |

A 3:1 reward-to-risk is a genuinely good ratio. You only need to be right 1 time in 4 to break even over a series of these. That's the kind of asymmetry that makes a trading edge work — *if* you actually take the stop when it hits.

## Position sizing — this is the part that protects your account

With $8,000, you size off the **dollar risk**, never off "how many shares can I afford."

| Account risk | $ at risk | Shares | Position cost | % of account | Profit at target |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1% | $80 | 267 | $1,467 | 18% | $240 |
| 2% (max) | $160 | 533 | $2,933 | 37% | $480 |

**Recommendation: risk 1%, not 2%.** On a low-float halt-prone runner, 1% is the disciplined choice because the *real* risk is larger than your stop suggests (see slippage below). Risking $80 to make $240 on an $8k account is a perfectly good trade. You do not need to be a hero here.

What you must NOT do: buy "as many shares as $8,000 allows" (≈1,455 shares). That turns a clean $80 loss into a ~$436 loss the moment it gaps through your stop — and on a stock like this, it can.

## The risks your plan hasn't accounted for

This is where a yes/no answer falls apart. Your setup looks clean, but a low-float biotech up 18% premarket on an FDA approval carries specific hazards:

1. **Premarket levels lie.** Your bull flag and "$5.50 breakout" are forming in *premarket*, on thin volume. Premarket structure frequently breaks down in the first 5–15 minutes of the regular session. A breakout you "see" at 9:15 may not exist at 9:35. Wait for the open and confirm the level holds with real volume.

2. **An 8M float means violent two-sided moves.** Low float is why it's up 18% — and it's exactly why it can drop 10% in a single candle. A $0.30 stop is *tiny* relative to how far this thing can travel in seconds. Your stop may not fill at $5.20. Realistic slippage could make your actual loss meaningfully bigger than $80–$160. That's the #1 reason to size at 1%.

3. **Gap-and-trap is the classic FDA-approval pattern.** "Buy the rumor, sell the news" is rampant on approvals. The 18% pop may already contain most of the move. A common sequence: spike at open → you buy the breakout → it reverses into a red day and grinds down. Ask: is volume still *coming in*, or did it peak premarket?

4. **Halt risk.** Fast small caps can trip volatility halts. If it halts against you, your stop does nothing until it reopens — potentially well below $5.20.

5. **"Relative volume 6x" and "looks like" — you're estimating.** Confirm the actual premarket volume and the catalyst details before risking real money. "Looks like 6x" is a feeling, not a level.

## The discipline checklist — answer these before you click buy

- [ ] Is the entry trigger an *actual* break of the regular-session level with volume confirmation — not a premarket line?
- [ ] Have you set a hard stop order at $5.20 *and* committed to honoring it (no "I'll give it room")?
- [ ] Did you size off 1% risk ($80 → ~267 shares), so a gap-through is survivable?
- [ ] Is this within your daily loss limit and your normal playbook — or are you chasing because it's "running"?
- [ ] What invalidates the thesis besides price? (e.g., volume dries up, it loses the flag, the open is weak.)
- [ ] Are you mentally prepared for the stock to be illiquid/halt and for your stop to slip?

## Verdict

The trade is **structurally takeable but not yet take-ready.** Your 3:1 R:R is good and your stop discipline is right — but you described a setup forming in *premarket*, and you're working off estimates ("looks like," "about 8M"). The setup needs to *prove itself* at the open before it earns your capital.

If you take it:
- **Size at 1% risk (~267 shares, ~$80 at risk)**, not 2% and absolutely not full buying power.
- **Wait for the regular-session open** and confirm the $5.50 level holds with real volume before entering. Don't buy the premarket line.
- **Honor the $5.20 stop mechanically.** The entire edge depends on your losses staying small. One trade where you "give it room" can erase ten disciplined ones.
- Accept that on an 8M-float runner, your real risk is somewhat larger than $80 due to slippage — which is exactly why you size small.

If you can't get a clean confirmed entry at the open, the correct move is to let it go. There is always another trade. Protecting the $8,000 is the job; this one position is not.
